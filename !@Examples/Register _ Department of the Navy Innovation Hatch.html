Your failure to follow these rules, whether listed below or in bulletins posted at various points in the website, may result in suspension or termination of your access to the website, without notice.

Purpose

The Hatch is a web-based community forum designed to enable innovation by soliciting your innovative ideas and suggestions to improve and enhance the Department of the Navy (DON). Your ideas and discussion may lead to improvements in DON efficiency, operations, readiness and ability to focus on “Warfighting First.” Ideas may include, but are not limited to: ways to enhance productivity and increase cost savings; policy changes to remove barriers or increase effectiveness; improvements to local or DON-wide processes and practices; and, suggestions to create new programs or initiatives, or to repurpose or improve current programs or initiatives. This site may not be used for the submission of any claim, demand, informal or formal complaint, or any other form of legal and/or administrative notice or process, or for the exhaustion of any legal and/or administrative remedy.

Registration

To participate in the Hatch, you must register. By registering for and using this site, you acknowledge your agreement with these terms of use. Use of this site is limited to active duty and reserve service members and DON civilian employees. Registration requires a valid government e-mail address, and users will be associated by their first and last name to moderators, but will be allowed to create unique usernames.

Comments

The Secretary of the Navy deeply values your time and input. The Hatch is a special forum for brainstorming and idea sharing. All users are considered innovators and should be treated with respect. All military and government employees are bound by all DON, DoD, and Government law and policies governing their conduct. Please ensure that your postings are appropriate and professional. Relevant lively discussion, debate, or disagreement is encouraged, but make a case for your point of view. Assume the good intentions of other posters, and treat all posters with respect. This site is intended to promote constructive discussions. The Hatch will be moderated, and all users will have the ability to flag any inappropriate submissions. Unprofessional, false, misleading, profane or defamatory comments are not appropriate for this site.

Posting Protocol

Make your submission count – your ideas should be based on facts and focused on our mission. Describe your idea using the time-tested “who-what-why-when-how” method. Describe what the idea is; what can be improved; for whom; how your idea will help the DON; when it should be implemented; what resources would be needed; what obstacles or challenges stand in the way of implementation, etc. You may not have all the answers necessary to solve a problem or fully develop your idea – collaboration among all innovators is encouraged to overcome this hurdle.

Your Ideas

You should expect that any idea may change based on the input of others. The Hatch is intended to harness the collective knowledge of DON innovators to create and refine the most promising ideas. Based on this collective dialogue, the DON may develop or implement proposals that are very different from the original concept. Any input may be valuable to find solutions to the challenges facing the DON.

Voting

The ideas posted on the Hatch are considered to be submissions by the individual poster, which will be evaluated on the quality of the idea and not the number of people who vote to agree or disagree with that idea. The voting option is used to help elevate ideas that are most popular.

Use of Measurement and Customization Technology

This website uses measurement and customization technology known as a "cookies." Cookies are used to remember a user's online interactions with a website or online application in order to conduct measurement and analysis of usage or to customize the user's experience.

There are two kinds of cookies used on this website. A single-session cookie (Tier 1) is a line of text that is stored temporarily on a user's computer and deleted as soon as the browser is closed. A persistent or multi-session cookie (Tier 2) is saved to a file on a user's hard drive and is called up the next time that user visits a website. Use of these cookies does not involve the collection of a visitor's PII.

The DON does not use the information associated with cookies to track individual user activity on the Internet outside DoD websites, nor does it share the data obtained through such technologies, without the user's explicit consent, with other departments or agencies. The DON does not keep a database of information obtained from the use of cookies.

Cookies are enabled by default to optimize website functionality and customize user experience. Users can choose not to accept the use of these cookies by changing the settings on their local computer's web browser. The USA.gov website, http://www.usa.gov/optout_instructions.shtml, provides general instructions on how to opt out of cookies and other commonly used web measurement and customization technologies.

Opting out of cookies still permits users to access comparable information and services; however, it may take longer to navigate or interact with the website if a user is required to fill out certain information repeatedly.
